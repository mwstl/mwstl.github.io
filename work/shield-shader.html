Situation

In the spring semester of 2021, I learned Processing during one of my courses. Nearing the end, I discovered the possibilities of generative artwork made with code. I thought, "Hey! This is pretty cool... how?" and thus began my journey into generative coding.

Process

I first started with finding inspiration and tutorials to jumpstart my journey.

One of my most referenced resources is The Coding Train who has released the popular book, The Nature of Code. I also found inspiration from OpenProcessing. Other resources have been referenced in the relevant posts on my Instagram.

I started off with tutorials to gain a more in-depth idea about how generative coding was different from the game-oriented coding I learned in my course.

Being relatively new to the world of coding, I didn't have a thorough understanding of why or how the code achieved certain results. However, for each project I did, I experimented and changed as much properties as I could, observing how one aspect would affect the final result.

As I went continued, I noticed trends such as using for loops to continually iterate, random operations to create different results, objects to create moving agents, and arrays to run them.

Of course, I encountered situations where I could not understand why the result wasn't matching the idea I had in mind. In addition, as I was coding in my free time, I sometimes felt pressed to upload before the end of the day every day (I did end up taking breaks here and there, but I made it eventually!).

In these situations, I created a brand new project and isolated the problem that I had. This allowed me to focus in and break down what I had at the moment, what could have gone wrong, what exactly I wanted to achieve, and how I could go about fixing the problem.

One of my favourite experiments (and arguably one that gave me the biggest headache), was the spherical flow field uploaded for day 61.

I had been working with flow fields for a while and I've used different versions of it. For this one, I inputted the object's current position into a noise operator to influence the next position.

However, the hard part was figuring out how to project 2D movement into a 3D shape and path.

As I was searching, I stumbled upon a the Cinder Creative Coding Cookbook which had a section on spherical flow fields. Although Cinder is in C++ and Processing is in Java, I was able to understand the basic idea behind creating spherical movement.

Once I got the main parts down, I played around with multiplying and dividing different values. Through this, I observed the dots moving in a figure-8 pattern, and changing certain values made the figure-8 elongate or squish.

At the end, I managed to finally achieve the idea I had in mind, learned a new algorithm, and most importantly, had fun!

Reflection

Over the course of the ~5 month project, I was able to learn a new skill and creative inspiration. It gave me a sense of reward, knowing that I could persevere and accomplish something like this. I was also able to work out my brain which had been dormant for a little while.
